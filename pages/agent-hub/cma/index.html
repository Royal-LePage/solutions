Strategy
Offer strategy
How we maximize your outcome.
Coordinated launch & offer holdback Pro strategy
List Thursday. Showings Friday through Monday. All offers presented Tuesday evening. This creates controlled urgency — buyers know others are looking, which drives competing offers and erodes conditions. We've used this model consistently across Mahogany and Auburn Bay with strong results.
Multiple offer presentation If we get there
When multiple offers arrive, you have choices — accept the best, counter one, or issue a call for highest and best. I present every offer in full, explain the terms and risks, and advise on strategy. You always make the final call. The goal is not just the highest price but the cleanest deal with the fewest conditions.
Bully offers Know this in advance
A bully offer arrives before the offer date with conditions designed to pressure you into accepting immediately. Alberta law requires me to present all offers. My advice in most cases: counter with a price that makes the disruption worth it, or ignore it and stick to the plan. We discuss this before the listing goes live so you're never caught off guard.
Subject-free vs. subject-to offers
A subject-free offer is cleaner and preferred, but don't dismiss a financed offer with a strong pre-approval and short conditions period. I'll vet every buyer's financing position before advising you on which offer to choose. A collapsed deal costs far more than a slightly lower clean offer.
Price reductions — if needed
If we don't receive an offer in the first 10 days, we revisit the data together. I'll show you updated showing feedback, DOM trends for competing listings, and any new sold comps. A targeted price reduction — not a panic cut — is a strategic tool, not a failure. We plan for this possibility before signing so you're mentally prepared.
