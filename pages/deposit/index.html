Pay Your Deposit

When you buy a home, your purchase contract will usually require you to pay a deposit.

The deposit shows the seller that you're serious about buying the property. It's held safely in the brokerage trust account and is later applied toward your purchase price when the sale closes. Royal LePage Solutions uses a secure online payment system called Payload to make paying your deposit easier, safer, and faster.

What Is the Deposit?

A deposit is money the buyer pays after the offer is accepted. It is not an extra fee — your deposit becomes part of your down payment or purchase funds when the deal closes.

For Example

If you buy a home and pay a $20,000 deposit, that $20,000 is credited toward the final amount you owe on possession day.

Where Does It Go?

Your deposit is paid into the Royal LePage Solutions trust account — a special bank account used to safely hold client money during a real estate transaction.

The money does not go to your REALTOR® personally, and it does not go to the seller right away. It's held safely until the transaction is completed, or until the contract says what should happen next.

The Process

How You Pay Your Deposit Online

1

You Receive the Deposit Link

Your REALTOR® will send you a secure deposit payment link. Before clicking or paying, make sure the link looks correct — a Royal LePage Solutions Payload link should come from a trusted source and take you to a secure Payload payment page.

If you are ever unsure, call your REALTOR® or Royal LePage Solutions before sending money.

2

You Enter the Property Details

The payment page will ask for some basic information so your deposit is connected to the correct property and transaction:

  • Your name
  • Your email address
  • The property address
  • The deposit amount
  • Your REALTOR®'s information
  • The listing REALTOR®'s information
3

You Choose How to Pay

Payload may offer several payment options, including a bank account payment, credit card, debit card, Apple Pay, or Google Pay. A direct bank payment is usually the best option, because it normally has the lowest fee.

4

You Review the Fee

Before you submit, Payload will show you any convenience fee. You see this fee before you confirm, so you can review it first. The full deposit amount still goes to the trust account — the fee is separate from the deposit.

5

You Submit the Payment

Once everything looks correct, you submit the payment. After it's processed, a receipt is emailed to you and the brokerage. The deposit usually takes about 2 business days to clear.

Security

Is Paying Through Payload Safe?

Yes. Payload is designed for secure real estate payments, and it helps protect buyers because the money is sent directly to the verified Royal LePage Solutions trust account.

  • Your money goes straight to a verified trust account.
  • You don't manually type in banking details — the trust account is already set up and verified.
  • It's safer than emailed wire instructions, which can be changed or faked by scammers.

What If the Deal Doesn't Go Through?

If the purchase doesn't move forward, the deposit does not automatically go to one side or the other. What happens depends on the purchase contract and the situation.

If everyone agrees the deposit should be returned, the brokerage can process the refund. If there's a disagreement between the buyer and seller, the brokerage may need written instructions from both sides — or a legal direction — before releasing the money.

This is normal in real estate, and is not specific to Payload.

Always Use a Verified Link

Only ever pay through a verified deposit link from your REALTOR®. If a link, email, or payment request looks wrong or unexpected in any way, do not send money — call your REALTOR® or Royal LePage Solutions directly to confirm first.

Quick Recap

Important Things to Remember

  • Your deposit is part of your purchase money.
  • It is held in the brokerage trust account.
  • It does not go directly to the seller right away.
  • You should only pay through a verified link.
  • You will receive a receipt after payment.
  • The deposit may take about 2 business days to clear.
